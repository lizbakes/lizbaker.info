Liz Baker

writer for the stage & screen

Liz Baker is a storyteller for theatre, film, and television. She holds a BA in Film Studies from Mount Holyoke College and is currently completing an MFA in Dramatic Writing at Carnegie Mellon University. She has worked with Kitchen Theatre Company, Hangar Theatre/Center for the Arts in Ithaca, Skyra Productions, Cape Playhouse, and MHC’s Project: Theatre. Most recently, her play The Death of Odysseus was produced as a part of the Theater Masters’ Take Ten Festival in Aspen, CO and New York, NY. It is set to be published by Samuel French in the Theater Masters’ Take Ten 2018 Anthology.

Scripts

Slip Into Her Skin

90 min (5w, 2m, 1n)

What do a fast-talking thirteen-year-old, an avant-garde theatre director, a trans accountant, and a devoutly-Christian actress have in common? An edgy warehouse production of Hedda Gabler draws together four radically different women as they struggle to exist in New York City. How do you untangle who you are when the world refuses to stop dictating what you should be?

Soaps Gold

30 min (2w, 2m)

Three cast members of soap opera Nouveau Riche are thrown for a loop when the execs elect to fire the entire production team and hire a highfalutin New York director to return the show to its daytime glory. Throw in a clandestine love affair and a dire diagnosis and soon reality starts to surpass the drama on TV.

The Death of Odysseus

10 min (2m)

Pete and Jasper have a pretty good thing going. Sure, they need to find a new roommate, but other than that, things are fine. That is, until Jasper makes it his mission to save the dying bee outside of their apartment. When a conversation about the ecological importance of bees takes an unexpected turn, the two roommates find themselves open to the scary possibility of much more.

Just-Fine in Rhode Island

90 min (3w, 2m)

Before she quit her PhD in astronomy, all Bea liked to do was look at the stars. Now, in her mid-twenties, she finds herself with no other interests, nothing to distract her from the failure of her dream, not the least of which being her boyfriend Lin who is a rising star in his grad department. When the two of them elect to escape to Rhode Island for the summer, Bea discovers the ultimate DIY project: her childhood friend Katy who just so happens to be in the middle of her own mental breakdown.

Porcupines

90 min (3w, 4m)

When she left home three years ago, Rose sort of expected that everything would stay the same. Now, after graduating college with zero prospects, she has returned to discover her sister has come out of the closet as her brother, her mother has moved in with another woman, and her father is unexpectedly in town. Oh yeah, and her ex-best friend is back home too. The only thing that hasn’t changed is Gramma Alma’s drinking, but that’s little comfort. When the tensions of this screwed-up blended family prove to be too much, Rose blows up and reveals the very secret that drove her from home in the first place, forcing everyone to decide where their loyalties lie.
